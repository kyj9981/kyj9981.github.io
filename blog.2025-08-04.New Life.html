New Life

Posted at August 04, 2025

Hey, it's me again with a bit of refined and clared mindset. Last time I talked about how perfectionism sucks and actually makes literally no grounds for progress and work.

Now, I'm not really obsessed in some meta philosophy or any particular insight. I believe they're an inherent part of me now; I'm just going to do my best and just keep going. I think the point is just doing your best, not comparing, being consistent, staying helathy, and not being afraid to fail. I read some interviews of various mathematicians and found something in common, which was that they are not afraid to fail. They just try whatever they can and approach a problem in various differet ways. That makes them super neuroplastic, creates positive feedback loops, and is also promising in general. Also important, I think, is identity. Now my new mindset would be consistent progresss; consistent hard work; a new person - I'm them, and they're me. Like "The Egg" by Andy Weir! One day, there will be a point that I will realize. Wait one last thing!! They never give up, because they're so optimistic. I learned recently that mathematicians tend to be optimistic - that's what keeps them going. And additionally I also have to chill and rest when I need to rest. I know best.

You've got this Yong Joon! I'm them, and they're me.
